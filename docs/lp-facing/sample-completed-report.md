view: what happened, which terms were triggered, which decisions require
approval, and what evidence should be shown before an LP or governance body is
asked to trust the process.

## What The Reviewer Should Inspect

1. Capital lifecycle: whether calls, payments, unfunded commitment, and reserve
   usage are understandable.
2. Governance packet: whether approvals, waivers, risks, and evidence are
   framed in a decision-ready way.
3. Fund terms / waterfall: whether economics and constraints are represented
   clearly enough for a second conversation.
4. Meeting pack: whether this could become useful material for LP updates,
   IC review, or LPAC discussion.
